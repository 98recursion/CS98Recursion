CS 1: Fall 2013

Short Assignment #8: Towers of Hanoi
Due: Friday, October 25

In class, we went over the Towers of Hanoi problem. In this short assignment, you will write a recursive function to solve the Towers of Hanoi problem. Your function must have the header

solve_hanoi(n, start_peg, end_peg)

This function moves all disks numbered 1 through n from peg start_peg to peg end_peg. It uses the remaining peg as a spare. Recall that the pegs are numbered 1, 2, and 3, and in the original problem, start_peg is 1 and end_peg is 2. (But in recursive calls, start_peg and end_peg could be any of 1, 2, and 3.)

The output of your function should give each move, saying for each move which disk moves from which peg to which peg. For example, here's my output for the call solve_hanoi(4, 1, 2):

Move disk 1 from peg 1 to peg 3.
Move disk 2 from peg 1 to peg 2.
Move disk 1 from peg 3 to peg 2.
Move disk 3 from peg 1 to peg 3.
Move disk 1 from peg 2 to peg 1.
Move disk 2 from peg 2 to peg 3.
Move disk 1 from peg 1 to peg 3.
Move disk 4 from peg 1 to peg 2.
Move disk 1 from peg 3 to peg 2.
Move disk 2 from peg 3 to peg 1.
Move disk 1 from peg 2 to peg 1.
Move disk 3 from peg 3 to peg 2.
Move disk 1 from peg 1 to peg 3.
Move disk 2 from peg 1 to peg 2.
Move disk 1 from peg 3 to peg 2.

Recall how to solve the problem:

  • The base case occurs when n = 1. Just move disk 1 from peg start_peg to peg end_peg.
  • In the recursive case, n ≥ 2. There are four things to do:
    1. Determine the number of peg spare_peg.
    2. Recursively solve the subproblem of moving disks 1 through n - 1 from peg start_peg to peg spare_peg. You will need to make a recursive call of the solve_hanoi function.
    3. Disk n is now exposed on peg start_peg, and peg end_peg has no disks smaller than disk n on it. Move disk n from peg start_peg to peg end_peg.
    4. Recursively solve the subproblem of moving disks 1 through n - 1 from peg spare_peg (where you put them in step 2) to peg end_peg. You will need to make a recursive call of the solve_hanoi function.

When solving the subproblems recursively, remember that when you call solve_hanoi, you pass it three parameters, and it tells you how to move disks 1 through the value of the first parameter from the peg whose number is given by the second parameter to the peg whose number is given by the third parameter, no matter how you denote these parameters at the point of call.

I was able to write the body of the solve_hanoi function in seven lines of Python, plus comments.

Submit your code, along with the output for the call solve_hanoi(5, 1, 2).
